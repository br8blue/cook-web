Biryani

Are you a fan of spice? Do you like rice? If you said yes to either one of those questions, maybe you should try biryani!











What is Biryani?

Biryani is a very popular food in many countries of South Asia. Some of these countries include India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Afghanistan, etc. It is made with rice, meat, some vegetables, and most importantly, spice! It can be eaten both as a vegetarian and non-vegetarian dish. For the most part, the meat people use for biryani include chicken, goat, lamb, etc. Also, sometimes people add eggs or potatoes, in the event that the person who is eating the meal is vegetarian.














Origin

The origins of biryani are actually quite a mystery! No one knows for sure, but some theories have circulated. However, the one that makes the most sense is the theory that it originated from Persia. The word "biryani" is actually derived from a Persian word, that word being "birian". Because of this, one can infer that biryani and Persian culture and cuisine must have strong ties. Plus, among foods in Persian cuisine, there are also other rice dishes like "pulao", and "pilaf" that were very popular. Another similar theory is that biryani came from chefs of the Mughal Empire, which was an Indian empire. The theory states that perhaps the people who first prepared biryani took inspiration from Persian culture and blended it with some Indian spices and other ingredients.






Etiquette

In several cultures, biryani is eaten with your right hand. Despite this though, many people around the world do eat it with utensils, like forks and spoons. If you have biryani and it is too spicy for you, you can always eat it with a side of raita! Raita is a dish that consists of a mixture of yogurt, vegetables and some herbs. The vegetables that are most commonly used include onions, cucumbers, and sometimes tomatoes.
