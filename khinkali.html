Khinkali

Do you like meat? Do you like cottage cheese? If you said yes to both, then you should try preparing Khinkali!











What is Khinkali?

Khinkali is a popular traditional dish in the country of Georgia, not to be confused with the state in the US. It is similar to a dumpling, as it is made of twisted dough. It is also stuffed with cottage cheese, meat, and some vegetables. The most commonly used meats for it include beef and pork, and people also put in mashed potato and mushrooms.














Origin

We've already established it's a traditional dish in Georgia (the nation). This may lead you to believe the food was actually created there, but that would be false! The earliest version of khinkali stems from the 13th century. Back then, the largest empire in the world was the Mongol Empire, which covered most of Asia and even some of Europe! Because of this, the Mongolians probably carried the idea of dumplings from China, and therefore took it with them all the way to Georgia, where the locals there learned what is was. The reason they took it to Georgia, was because it just so happened to be along the Silk Road, one of the most important routes of trade back then. It was also because Mongolian conquerers would stuff their meat in dough, this way the meat would be conserved better while horse-riding.






Etiquette

Not all foods are meant to be consumed the same, so what about khinkali? Like most food, khinkali has rules that apply to it when eating in a respectful manner. When the meat is stuffed into the dumpling, it is put in raw. That way, since the meat's already inside, the khinkali and all the ingredients will be cooked with it, and the juices will be trapped within the dough. To make your khinkali juicier, many people add warm water or even broth to the meat. Also, you should never use a fork for eating khinkali. Using a fork will pierce into the dumpling and cause all the juices to spill out. You may also notice that at the top of the khinkali, there are knots. These knots are not meant to be eaten.
