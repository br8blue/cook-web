Paella

Would you enjoy a meal with a combination of rice, meat, vegetables, and much more? If so, Paella is right for you!











What is Paella?

Paella is an extremely well known rice dish that originated in Valencia, a region in Spain. It's also considered symbolic of Valencian and Spanish culture. It consists of rice, meat, and vegetables. The meats involved include chicken and rabbit. Also, some times sea food is added as well. As for vegetables, green beans and lima beans are added.













Origin

In the 900s, people in Al-Andalus starting farming rice very frequently. Due to this mass production of rice, locals near the Iberian Peninsula starting preparing and eating lots of rice, fish, and spices for both family and religious gatherings. Due to how significant and regular this become to people back then, by the 1400s, rice became an iconic staple of Spanish agriculture and cuisine. As for it's name, it means "frying pan" in Valencian due to the fact that it was commonly cooked in a hollow frying pan.






Etiquette

Unlike a lot of other foods, paella has relatively simple etiquette that must be respected when eating. For starters, on the bottom layer of the casserole, there is something called "socarrat". The socarrat is considered the best part, and is therefore conserved and equally served to everyone at the dinner table. Also, you can eat paella with any utensil, but it's traditionally consumed with a spoon, usually wooden. However, there is one rule that isn't very simple or obvious to some people. The rule about the pan. In Valencia, paella is eaten directly from the serving pan or casserole, so there is no need for plates.
